Fundamentals

Welcome to νTeX — an interactive platform for mastering LaTeX. Work through the exercises to build fluency one expression at a time.

Inline and Display Math

Inline math uses \(...\); display math uses \[...\]. The platform handles wrappers automatically.

Basic Operators and Symbols

Core operators: +, -, \times, \div, =, \neq. Relations: \leq, \geq, \approx.

Greek Letters

Greek letters are commands: \alpha, \beta, \gamma, giving \(\alpha+\beta=\gamma\).

Fractions, Binomials, Roots, and Radicals

Commands for fractions, binomial coefficients, and roots.

Fractions

Use \frac{num}{den}, e.g. \(\frac{a+b}{c}\). For inline contexts, consider \tfrac.

Square Roots and Radicals

\sqrt{x} gives \(\sqrt{x}\). For nth roots: \sqrt[n]{x} gives \(\sqrt[3]{x}\).

Binomial Coefficients

\binom{n}{k} gives \(\binom{n}{k}\).

Alphabets, Fonts, and Text

Various font styles and alphabets for mathematical notation.

Math Fonts

\mathbf, \mathit, \mathrm, \mathsf change font style inside math mode.

Calligraphic and Script

\mathcal{A} gives \(\mathcal{A}\).

Blackboard Bold and Fraktur

\mathbb{R} gives \(\mathbb{R}\). Use \mathfrak{g} for Fraktur.

Bracket Sizing

Use \left and \right before a delimiter to scale it automatically:

\left( \frac{a}{b} \right)
\left\{ x \middle| x > 0 \right\}

The \middle command creates a scaling delimiter between a \left/\right pair.

Declaring Paired Delimiters

In full LaTeX (with the mathtools package), define custom delimiter commands:

\DeclarePairedDelimiter{\abs}{\lvert}{\rvert}
\DeclarePairedDelimiter{\norm}{\lVert}{\rVert}
\DeclarePairedDelimiter{\floor}{\lfloor}{\rfloor}
\DeclarePairedDelimiter{\ceil}{\lceil}{\rceil}

After declaration, \abs{x} gives \(|x|\), \norm{v} gives \(\|v\|\), and starred variants auto-scale.

KaTeX does not support \DeclarePairedDelimiter directly, but understanding the concept prepares you for real LaTeX documents.
